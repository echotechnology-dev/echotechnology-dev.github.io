Why I Build Browsers

My name is Max — an independent, self-taught developer. I never planned to build a browser until I began to understand how much personal data modern browsers collect — often without transparency or meaningful consent.

How It Started

The deeper I looked into browser telemetry — especially inside Google Chrome — the clearer it became that modern browsers are no longer neutral tools. Not only technically, but ethically.

I refused to accept a web where privacy is a luxury and users are treated as exploitable datasets. That decision became the starting point for Echo.

Why I Build Echo

I do not work for corporations or investors. Echo has no ads, no crypto incentives, and no participation in the surveillance economy.

I build Echo because I believe software can be honest. Tools should serve people — not advertisers.

What Echo Represents

Echo is not a product. It is a position.

Echo is a principled response to a digital ecosystem where surveillance has become normalized and data extraction is treated as standard practice.

Echo proves that browsers can respect users without sacrificing performance or usability. No ads. No tracking. No background services.

Autonomy, transparency and respect for the individual are not ideals here — they are requirements.

My Goal

To create browsers that respect users, avoid tracking, collect nothing, do not sell behavior, and remain fast even on older or low-power hardware.

Privacy should not depend on expensive devices or technical expertise.

Where This Is Going

Surf is only the beginning. More tools will follow — not to dominate the market, but to prove that ethical alternatives exist. Simpler. Lighter. More human.

If you value privacy, simplicity and honest software — you’re welcome here.

— Max
